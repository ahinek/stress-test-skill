Initiative: Consolidation of multiple independent Jira workspaces into a single centralized workspace with AI tool integration

Executive Summary

Frame: It is six months from now. The Jira consolidation has failed catastrophically. AI tools are creating tickets in wrong locations, breaking cross-org workflows. Partner organization connections are severed. Customer Service cannot route bugs. Security issues are being mis-classified. The initiative is being emergency-rolled back while teams scramble to restore critical external integrations.

Most Likely Failure

  • Failure: AI tools land output in wrong locations after naming convention changes
  • Why: AI tools were trained/configured on old workspace structure (project keys, field IDs, board names); migration changes these identifiers without updating AI tool configurations; AI creates tickets in wrong projects or with malformed field mappings
  • Assumption: AI tools will adapt automatically to new workspace structure, or someone will remember to update all AI tool configurations before cutover

Most Dangerous Failure

  • Failure: Partner organization integrations break, severing critical cross-org workflows
  • Why: External partners have hard-coded dependencies to current workspace structure (API endpoints, project keys, webhook URLs); migration breaks these silently; partner teams discover breakage when critical escalations fail to route

Cheapest Failure to Prevent

  • Failure: Customer Service bug allocation and Security issue escalation routing breaks
  • Action: Map all Customer Service and Security routing rules before migration; test in parallel workspace for 2 weeks; maintain dual-routing during 30-day cutover period (cost: 2-3 weeks, ~$20K vs. weeks of mis-routed critical issues)

Hidden Assumption

  • Assumption: "AI tools successfully and accurately land output in correct locations" appears in success criteria but has no corresponding validation, testing, or rollback plan—success is assumed, not engineered

Pre-Launch Checklist

  • AI tool validation: All AI tools tested against new workspace structure; configurations updated; field mappings validated; output landing in correct locations confirmed
  • Partner notification: All partner organizations notified 60+ days in advance; new API endpoints/webhook URLs provided; partner teams have test access; integration testing completed jointly
  • Customer Service routing: Bug allocation rules mapped to new workspace; tested end-to-end for 2+ weeks; dual-routing established; Customer Service team trained on new structure
  • Security escalation: Vulnerability and exposure issue workflows mapped and tested; escalation routing validated; Security team confirms issue creation and routing works correctly
  • Naming convention mapping: Identifier mapping table created (old → new); all known hard-coded references inventoried and updated; deprecation warnings in place for old identifiers
  • Parallel workspace testing: Production-like parallel workspace running for 2-4 weeks; AI tools, partner integrations, Customer Service, Security, and internal automation tested; top 20 issues resolved
  • Dual-routing cutover: 30-day dual-access period planned (old workspace read-only, routing mirrors to both); rollback plan tested (< 4 hour reactivation of old workspace)
