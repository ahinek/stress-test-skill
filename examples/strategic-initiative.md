# Example: Strategic Initiative Stress Test

Prompt:

Stress test this strategic initiative:

Plan:
We are launching an organization-wide AI acceleration initiative intended to improve delivery speed, reduce operational friction, and increase engineering productivity across multiple product areas over the next 12 months.

The initiative includes:
- expanded AI tooling access
- AI-assisted development workflows
- increased automation expectations
- leadership reporting on AI adoption
- operational guidance for teams
- enablement and training sessions

Audience / stakeholders:
Engineering teams, product management, quality engineering, release management, engineering leadership, and executive leadership.

Success criteria:
- measurable increase in delivery throughput
- improved delivery predictability
- reduced manual operational overhead
- visible AI adoption across teams
- maintained or improved software quality
- reduced cycle time for feature delivery

Constraints:
- existing delivery commitments remain unchanged
- teams already report capacity pressure
- tooling maturity varies significantly between groups
- quality expectations remain high
- some workflows are poorly documented today
- cross-functional dependencies already create friction

Known concerns:
- AI may accelerate unstable processes rather than improve them
- teams may optimize for visible AI usage instead of meaningful outcomes
- leadership expectations may outpace operational readiness
- inconsistent tooling and workflows may create fragmentation
- enablement may not keep pace with adoption pressure

Please provide:
- Most likely failure
- Most dangerous failure
- Cheapest failure to prevent
- Hidden assumption
- Base-rate reasoning
- Organizational failure mode analysis
- Revised plan recommendations
- Pre-launch checklist
- Early warning indicators
