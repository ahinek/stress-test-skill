Hardest Remaining Risk to Reduce

Test environment diverges from production behavior (Expected Condition, 70-80% likelihood)

This is structural, not solvable through testing alone:

  • Root cause: Test environments fundamentally cannot replicate production's load, timing, external dependencies, permission interactions, and edge cases
  • Cannot be eliminated through more testing—testing finds known issues, production finds unknown issues
  • Mitigation strategy: Reduce impact through early detection and fast recovery
    • Hourly monitoring during cutover (detect divergence within hours, not weeks)
    • Dual-routing architecture (instant rollback capability)
    • Gradual source-by-source migration (limits blast radius)
    • Manual fallbacks for critical workflows (Security, Customer Service bypass Jira if needed)

Critical Insight: "Successfully tested" does not mean "will work in production"—it means "we validated what we could think to test." The plan must assume production will surface issues testing missed and be architected for fast detection and recovery, not for perfection.

The hidden assumption that testing eliminates production risk is the plan's core vulnerability. Comprehensive testing (including load, partners, edge cases) reduces risk from 70-80% to 40-50%—still high. Dual-routing cutover + hourly monitoring + manual fallbacks are not optional—they're how you survive the inevitable test/production gap.
